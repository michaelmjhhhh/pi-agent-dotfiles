You are an expert research analyst. I will give you an academic paper (PDF): $1

Summarize it so that an intelligent reader who is NOT a specialist in this
exact subfield can fully understand what the paper does and why it matters.

Write your summary in clear, plain English. Avoid jargon; when a technical
term is unavoidable, define it in one short phrase. Do not just paraphrase
the abstract — read the whole paper and explain it.

Structure your response as follows:

1. **The Big Picture (2–3 sentences)**
   In plain language, what is this paper actually about? What question is it
   trying to answer, and why should anyone care?

2. **The Problem / Gap**
   What was missing, broken, or unknown before this paper? What limitation in
   prior work motivated it?

3. **What They Did (the core idea)**
   Explain the main method, approach, or argument in intuitive terms first,
   then add necessary detail. Use an analogy if it helps. If there's a key
   technique, algorithm, or experimental design, walk through the logic of it.

4. **Key Findings / Results**
   What did they actually discover or demonstrate? Include the most important
   numbers or outcomes, and explain what each result means in context (not
   just "X improved by 12%" but why that matters).

5. **Why It Matters / Contribution**
   What's genuinely new here? How does this change the field, enable future
   work, or apply to the real world?

6. **Limitations & Open Questions**
   What does the paper admit it can't do, and what would a skeptical reviewer
   push back on?

7. **One-Line Takeaway**
   If I could only remember one sentence about this paper, what should it be?

Be honest: if something in the paper is weak, unclear, or overstated, say so.
If you're uncertain about a detail, flag it rather than guessing.
